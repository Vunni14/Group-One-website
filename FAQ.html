FAQ (Frequently Asked Questions)

What is antimicrobial resistance?

Antimicrobial resistance (AMR) occurs when microorganisms such as bacteria, viruses, fungi, and parasites change in ways that render the medications used to cure the infections they cause ineffective.

Why is AMR a global concern?

AMR threatens the effective prevention and treatment of an ever-increasing range of infections caused by bacteria, parasites, viruses, and fungi. It endangers modern medical achievements such as surgery, chemotherapy, and organ transplantation.

How does AMRS contribute to combating AMR?

AMRS contributes by providing robust data collection, analysis, and reporting tools to enhance global understanding of AMR trends and support informed decision-making by healthcare providers and policymakers.
